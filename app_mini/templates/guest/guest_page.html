ABOUT US

The Kerala Police, with its roots tracing back to the Travancore State Police of 1936, officially came into being on November 1, 1956, with Shri. N. Chandrasekharan Nair IPS as its first Inspector General. Guided by the Sanskrit motto "Mridhu Bhave Dhrida Kruthye" – "Soft in Temperament, Firm in Action" – the force is dedicated to protecting life and property with honesty and impartiality, striving for high citizen satisfaction and upholding the rule of law under the leadership of the State Police Chief, currently Shaikh Darvesh Sahib, IPS. Operating under the Home Department of the Kerala Government and organized into South and North Zones, Ranges, Districts, and Commissionerates, the Kerala Police has a distinguished history, including the establishment of India's first women's police station in 1973 and the elevation of its head to DGP in 1981. Renowned for effective law enforcement and innovative community policing initiatives like "Janamaithri," the Kerala Police has also been at the forefront of technological advancements with projects like Cyberdome and online safety initiatives such as "Aparajitha is Online" and "KIDGLOVE," demonstrating a commitment to both traditional policing values and modern challenges while actively engaging in social welfare programs for the community.

“The "Excellence in Cyber Security: Cyberdome Initiative" achievement highlights the Kerala Police's significant strides in tackling the growing threat of cybercrime through its technological research and development center, Cyberdome. This initiative has garnered national recognition, exemplified by the FICCI SMART Policing Award in 2018 for Cyber Security, underscoring Cyberdome's effective public-private partnerships and proactive measures. Its successes include establishing a Social Media Lab to address online challenges, hosting the international COCON conference for knowledge sharing, conducting crucial security assessments for government websites, providing vital cyber security training, actively monitoring the darknet, and developing innovative tools like the Piracy Tracker. Furthermore, Cyberdome's expansion with new wings and its development of child safety programs like "KIDGLOVE" and advanced tracking software demonstrate its commitment to a comprehensive and evolving approach to cyber security, making it a notable achievement for the Kerala Police. ”

“ The Kerala Police's commendable achievement in disaster response is significantly bolstered by their well-established and effective coordination mechanisms with various crucial entities. This involves seamless collaboration with other government agencies, ensuring clear communication channels for rapid information exchange and joint planning through pre-disaster training exercises that define distinct roles and responsibilities. Furthermore, their integration into centralized command and control structures during crises allows for strategic decision-making and efficient resource allocation alongside the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF). Recognizing the vital role of community participation, the Kerala Police also actively collaborates with volunteer groups, integrating their efforts into the overall response strategy. This cohesive and collaborative approach ensures a unified and impactful response during natural calamities, maximizing their ability to save lives and provide aid effectively. ”
